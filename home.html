What is the Digital Divide?

T he Digital Divide refers to the gap between those who have access to modern technology and those who do not. It includes access to internet connectivity, digital devices, and the skills required to use them effectively.

In today's digital age, access to technology determines academic success, economic mobility, and social participation. Without equal access, inequality continues to widen across communities.
